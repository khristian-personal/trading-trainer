Did price break above ORH or below ORL on a clearly elevated volume candle?

The breakout candle should be noticeably larger in volume than the candles inside the range. If volume is light, this may be a fakeout — don't proceed.

4

Pullback in Progress

Is price returning toward the breakout level without fully re-entering the opening range?

For longs: price pulled back toward ORH but hasn't closed back below it. For shorts: price pulled back toward ORL but hasn't closed back above it. If price re-enters the OR, the setup is invalid.

5

Pullback Volume

Is the volume on the pullback candles lighter than the breakout candle?

Light pullback volume = healthy retracement. Sellers are not aggressively pushing against the move. Heavy pullback volume = potential reversal; the breakout may have been absorbed.

6

Confirmation Candle

Has a rejection candle formed at the retest level?

Look for: a long lower wick (for longs) showing buyers defended the level, a bullish engulfing candle where buyers overwhelmed sellers, or a pin bar. If price is still falling into the level, wait for the candle to close.
